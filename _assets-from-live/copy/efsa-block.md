# EFSA Authority Block

## Suggested copy (US market — EFSA reference acceptable as "European regulator" wording)

- **Title** : EFSA-backed fiber
- **Sub / Regulation** : EU Regulation 432/2012 · ID 3120
- **Quote** : "konjac glucomannan supports satiety in the context of an energy-restricted diet" — officially recognized by the European Food Safety Authority.

## Style intent
- Background : `--lavender-soft` (#E8DCEF)
- Centered layout
- White badge container with check icon
- Quote in italic serif (Times) — emphasizes authority

## Compliance notes
- ✅ EFSA claim is **only allowed** for konjac glucomannan in 3g daily dose context with adequate water intake. The brief uses correct wording.
- ⚠️ "EFSA" branding is EU-specific. US shoppers may not recognize. Consider adding a clarifier like "the European equivalent of the FDA" — but the FDA itself never "approves" supplements, so don't equate.
- ❌ DO NOT translate the quote freely — only the official EFSA-approved wording is legally usable.
- ✅ "officially recognized by the European Food Safety Authority" is factually true and safer than "EFSA-approved" or "EFSA-certified" (both imprecise).

## Alternative US framing (if EFSA feels foreign)
> Konjac glucomannan : a soluble fiber recognized by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA Regulation 432/2012, ID 3120) as contributing to satiety when 3g is consumed daily with water before meals.
